About Project

The H-1B visa program in the United States serves as a vital conduit for skilled foreign professionals to augment the nation's workforce. With a dataset spanning from 2017 to 2022, comprising 3.4 million data points sourced from the USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub, this project endeavors to delve deep into the intricacies of visa approvals. Our objective is twofold: firstly, to identify the pivotal factors influencing H-1B visa grants and, secondly, to develop a predictive model that forecasts approval outcomes. By gaining insights into these determinants, we aim to not only optimize the visa application process but also offer invaluable guidance to applicants and employers, thereby enhancing their chances of securing successful visa grants.

Furthermore, this project is motivated by the multifaceted role of the H-1B visa program. Beyond its significance in bridging skill gaps across various industries, analyzing this data sheds light on evolving immigration policies and socioeconomic dynamics. Additionally, the development of a predictive model holds immense practical value, serving as a strategic tool for stakeholders navigating the intricate landscape of H-1B visa applications. Ultimately, by understanding and leveraging these insights, we aim to streamline decision-making processes and empower stakeholders with actionable information to enhance their approach to visa applications.
