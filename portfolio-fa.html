About Project

The financial analytics project, titled "Exploring CAPM with SP500 stock's Beta Exposure using R," takes you on a journey into the world of finance and data analysis. Leveraging the power of R, a versatile data analysis tool, we investigate the Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) and its practical applications. We start by collecting historical stock price data for IBM and the market index, followed by rigorous data cleaning and transformation to ensure data accuracy. Then, we use predictive modeling through linear regression in R to compute IBM's beta, a vital risk assessment metric for investors.

In this project, I demonstrate how data acquisition, data cleaning, and data transformation are integral steps in financial analytics. I sourced and refined the data to build a robust foundation for our analysis. The predictive modeling phase, using R's powerful statistical capabilities, enables us to estimate IBM's beta exposure to the market. This project not only equips you with an understanding of the CAPM but also showcases the practical use of data analysis tools in the field of finance, empowering you with the skills to make informed investment decisions and optimize your portfolio management strategies.
